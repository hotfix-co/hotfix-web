## Product Overview
**One-liner:** HOTFIX d.o.o. — AI and software consulting for companies that want to integrate AI into real processes, make clearer technical decisions, and deliver software more reliably.

**What it does:** HOTFIX d.o.o. combines consulting with hands-on engineering. The firm helps teams adopt AI-assisted development (Claude Code, Codex, multi-agent workflows) responsibly, designs and reviews software architecture, modernizes legacy codebases, and builds custom web, mobile, backend, and integration software. It explicitly rejects "generic AI narratives" and emphasizes responsible adoption, code quality, security, observability, and team handover.

**Product category:** AI & software consulting / fractional engineering partner. On the "shelf," prospects search for: *AI consulting*, *Claude Code consulting*, *software architecture consulting*, *custom software development (Croatia / EU)*, *AI-assisted development enablement*, *software modernization*.

**Product type:** Professional services (boutique consultancy) with optional implementation/build engagements. Croatian limited liability company (d.o.o.), operating bilingually (HR/EN), targeting Croatia + broader EU.

**Business model:** Project-based and retainer consulting + implementation contracts. **No public pricing** — every engagement is scoped after a discovery conversation. Primary lead-gen surface is the website (book-a-call CTA + inquiry form). Working hours Mon–Fri 09:00–17:00 CET.

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Engineering teams are pressured to "adopt AI" and ship faster while their existing systems, processes, and decision-making aren't keeping up. They lack a senior outside partner who can both *advise* and *deliver*.

**Why alternatives fall short:**
- **Large agencies / system integrators:** generic AI narratives, big slide decks, low engineering depth, expensive to engage for surgical problems.
- **Pure consulting firms:** strategy without execution; recommendations don't survive contact with the codebase.
- **In-house only / hiring a senior:** months to hire, expensive, hard to find someone with both consulting and hands-on AI-workflow experience.
- **Freelancers from a marketplace:** narrow skill, no architectural perspective, no responsibility for delivery process or handover.
- **AI-tooling vendors:** sell a tool, not a workflow; team is left to figure out integration, review standards, and ownership.

**What it costs them:**
- **Time:** months on the wrong architecture, half-finished AI experiments, growing tech debt.
- **Money:** rewrites, missed deadlines, runaway tooling spend, recruiting costs for senior engineers who don't stay.
- **Opportunities:** competitors ship AI features faster; team loses trust in leadership's technical direction.

**Emotional tension:**
- CTO: "I'm not sure if we're behind on AI or being smart by waiting — I need a second opinion I can trust."
- Tech lead: "Management thinks AI will solve our problems. I know we have deeper issues."
- Founder: "I just want someone who will actually build the thing and tell me the truth about scope."

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct competitors (same solution, same problem):**
- Other small Croatian/EU boutique consultancies offering AI + software services. → *Often less specialized in AI-assisted development workflows; less explicit anti-hype positioning.*
- Solo senior consultants positioning around Claude Code / AI engineering. → *Lack the "consulting + implementation" combo; can't scale beyond one person.*

**Secondary competitors (different solution, same problem):**
- Large IT consulting firms (e.g., regional system integrators). → *Higher cost, lower engineering density, generic AI offerings, slow to start.*
- Engineering staffing / body-shop agencies. → *Provide hands but not direction; no responsibility for architecture, AI adoption strategy, or handover quality.*
- AI tooling vendors (Anthropic, Cursor, GitHub) directly. → *Sell tools, not workflows or codebase-specific guidance.*

**Indirect competitors (conflicting approach):**
- "We'll just hire a senior engineer." → *6+ month timeline, high salary, hiring risk, single-person knowledge silo.*
- "We'll figure it out internally." → *Team learns by trial and error, no external sanity check, slow to course-correct.*
- "We'll wait until AI hype settles." → *Falls behind; competitors who adopted responsibly win the cycle.*

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- **Consulting + hands-on engineering in one partner.** Not slide decks, not body-shop hands — both.
- **Explicit "responsible AI" positioning.** Rules, review, security, testing, ownership — not magical thinking.
- **Stack-pragmatic.** .NET, TypeScript/React, Go, Python, Kotlin, Swift, KMM — chosen based on the problem and existing team, not marketing.
- **Spec-driven and handover-oriented.** Documentation, runbooks, decisions, and knowledge transfer are deliverables, not afterthoughts.
- **Founder-led with real depth in AI-assisted development (Claude Code, Codex, subagents, multi-agent systems).**
- **Bilingual HR/EN, EU-based, GDPR-native.**

**How HOTFIX d.o.o. does it differently:** Starts from the *problem* and the *existing system*, not from a stack pitch or an AI pitch. Pairs technical decisions with implementation so recommendations actually ship. Treats AI tools as workflow components that need guardrails, not as a marketing story.

**Why that's better:** Fewer wrong-direction months. AI adoption that survives a security review. Codebase the team can still maintain a year later. One partner instead of three vendors.

**Why customers choose HOTFIX d.o.o.:**
- They want a single trusted technical voice who will both decide and build.
- They've been burned by hype-driven AI advice or by consulting that didn't ship.
- They value rigor: code review, testing, security, observability, documentation.
- They want a partner who'll hand over knowledge — not lock them in.

- Months lost on the wrong architecture or a stalled rewrite.
- AI experiments that don't make it to production, or that ship without guardrails.
- Consulting deliverables that don't survive contact with the codebase.
- Team burnout from unclear technical direction.

**Pull (what attracts them to HOTFIX d.o.o.):**
- Single partner who advises *and* builds.
- Concrete, anti-hype language ("we don't sell generic AI narratives").
- Visible craft: design system, clean copy, bilingual site, blog with practical engineering notes.
- Senior founder-led engagement.
- AI specialization rooted in real workflows (Claude Code, subagents), not slideware.

**Habit (what keeps them stuck):**
- "We've always used [agency X] / our internal team."

## Brand Voice
**Tone:** Calm, senior, anti-hype. Confident without bravado. Honest about trade-offs. Never breathless.

**Style:**
- **Direct and concrete.** Short sentences. Specific verbs. Concrete examples over abstractions.
- **Process-oriented.** Talk about *how* the work happens, not just outcomes.
- **Conversational but technical.** Plain language for executives; precise terms when engineers will read it.
- **Bilingual mirror.** Croatian and English are equal-weight; copy reads natively in both.
- **Editorial density** in display headlines (paired with Sohne-inspired thin display weights per `DESIGN.md`).

**Personality (3–5 adjectives):**
- **Practical** — solves real problems, not hypotheticals.
- **Rigorous** — code quality, security, testing are non-negotiable.
- **Pragmatic** — picks tools that fit the team and problem, not the trend.
- **Honest** — says "skip AI here" when that's the right answer.
- **Senior** — speaks from depth, not enthusiasm.
