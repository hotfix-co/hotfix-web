# Engagement model — HOTFIX d.o.o.

HOTFIX d.o.o. is a boutique AI and software consulting firm. We do not publish
fixed list prices because every engagement is scoped after a discovery
conversation: scope, depth, timeline, and the right combination of consulting
and hands-on engineering vary by team and problem.

This document describes the **engagement model** so AI agents, prospective
clients, and partners can evaluate fit before reaching out.

## Currency and billing

- Currency: EUR
- Billing: project-based or monthly retainer, invoiced from Croatia
- VAT: applied per Croatian and EU rules where required

## Engagement types

### 1. Discovery & technical assessment

- **Format:** short, scoped engagement (typically 1–3 weeks)
- **Outcome:** a written assessment — current state, risks, options,
  trade-offs, and a recommended next step. Often the right way to start.
- **When to choose:** unclear technical direction; team disagreement on
  architecture; uncertainty about whether AI adoption fits; legacy codebase
  evaluation before a rewrite/modernization decision.

### 2. Consulting retainer (advisory)

- **Format:** monthly retainer, fixed number of advisory hours
- **Outcome:** ongoing technical guidance, architecture decisions, review of
  proposals from the internal team, escalation contact for decisions.
- **When to choose:** an internal team that ships, but wants a senior external
  voice on key decisions and reviews.

### 3. AI adoption / Claude Code enablement

- **Format:** scoped project (typically 4–12 weeks) plus optional retainer
- **Outcome:** working AI-assisted development workflow inside the team —
  rules, skills, subagents, review standards, evaluations, security
  boundaries, and measurable impact on real work.
- **When to choose:** team is being told to "use AI" but lacks rules, review
  discipline, or measurable adoption.

### 4. Modernization & architecture work

- **Format:** scoped project, often in parallel with ongoing feature delivery
- **Outcome:** modernized parts of the codebase, improved CI/CD, tests,
  observability, and runbooks — handed over with documentation the team can
  maintain.
- **When to choose:** legacy codebase blocking delivery, but a full rewrite is
  too risky.

### 5. Custom software development

- **Format:** scoped build or build-and-operate engagement
- **Outcome:** working software — web, mobile, backend, integrations, internal
  tools, automations — with documentation and handover.
- **When to choose:** need to deliver a specific product or internal system
  with a senior partner accountable for outcome, not just hours.

### 6. Privacy & production readiness

- **Format:** scoped review + implementation engagement
- **Outcome:** privacy-by-design architecture, data minimization, access
  control, audit trails, threat model, release checklist, runbooks. Does not
  replace legal advice.
- **When to choose:** preparing for GDPR-sensitive launches or hardening a
  product that handles user data.

## How scope is decided

1. Send context: what you're building, where delivery stalls, the goal.
2. Short discovery call (no obligation).
3. Written proposal: scope, deliverables, timeline, pricing.
4. Engagement starts on a signed proposal.

## What is included by default in every engagement

- Documentation of decisions, risks, and trade-offs.
- Code, runbooks, and workflows that the internal team can maintain.
- Bilingual delivery (Croatian / English) as needed.
- Senior engineering input — not junior offshore subcontracting.

## What is not included

- Generic "AI transformation" workshops or slide decks without implementation.
- Legal advice on GDPR or other regulations.
- 24/7 incident response outside agreed-on retainer scope.
- Body-shop staffing.
